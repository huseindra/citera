# Clinical Study Protocol — ONC-450

**Study Title:** A Phase 3, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Zelparib as Maintenance Therapy in Adults with Advanced Ovarian Cancer

**Sponsor:** Aruna Therapeutics, Inc. | **Protocol Version:** 4.0

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## 1. Objectives

To evaluate the efficacy of Zelparib 300 mg twice daily versus placebo on progression-free survival in adults with advanced high-grade serous ovarian cancer who have responded to first-line platinum-based chemotherapy. Zelparib is an investigational PARP inhibitor; it has not been approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

## 2. Study Design

Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group trial at 42 sites. Approximately **420 participants** will be randomized **2:1** to Zelparib or matching placebo as maintenance therapy. Treatment continues until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity; a safety follow-up occurs 30 days after the last dose.

## 3. Study Procedures

Participants attend clinic visits every 4 weeks. Each visit includes vital signs, physical examination, a symptom review, and a venous blood draw of approximately 20 mL for hematology and safety chemistry. Imaging (CT) is performed every 12 weeks to assess disease status.

## 4. Participant Responsibilities

Participants must take the study drug twice daily, complete the symptom diary, attend scheduled visits and imaging, and report new symptoms promptly to the study team.

## 5. Risks and Safety Considerations

In earlier studies, Zelparib caused **myelosuppression**: anemia (Grade 3 or higher in ~18% of participants), neutropenia (~9%), and thrombocytopenia (~6%), managed by dose interruption and transfusion. **Complete blood counts are monitored at every visit.** A small number of participants developed **myelodysplastic syndrome / acute myeloid leukemia (MDS/AML), a serious and potentially fatal condition.** Common adverse events include nausea (fatigue), fatigue, and decreased appetite.

## 6. Benefits

Participants may experience a longer period without cancer progression; benefit is not guaranteed, and one-third of participants receive placebo. The information learned may help future patients with ovarian cancer.

## 7. Alternative Treatments

Alternatives to participation include routine surveillance (observation) after chemotherapy, or other approved maintenance therapies where eligible. Your oncologist can discuss these options and their benefits and risks.

## 8. Compensation for Injury

The sponsor maintains clinical trial insurance. For a trial-related injury, **the sponsor will pay the reasonable costs of medical treatment**, in accordance with the site's compensation policy.

## 9. Confidentiality and Record Access

Records identifying participants are kept confidential. Authorized monitors, auditors, the IRB, and the **FDA may inspect original medical records** to verify study conduct.

## 10. Contacts

Participants may contact the principal investigator for study questions, the IRB for questions about their rights, and a 24-hour line for trial-related injury.

## 11. Voluntary Participation

Participation is voluntary; participants may decline or withdraw at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which they are otherwise entitled.
