# Informed Consent Form — Study ONC-450

**Study Title:** A Phase 3, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Zelparib as Maintenance Therapy in Adults with Advanced Ovarian Cancer

**Sponsor:** Aruna Therapeutics, Inc. | **Consent Version:** 3.1

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## Introduction

You are being asked to take part in a research study. This form explains the study so you can decide whether to participate. Please read it carefully and ask the study team any questions before you sign.

## Why is this study being done?

This study involves research. Its purpose is to find out whether an investigational medicine called Zelparib, taken as maintenance therapy after chemotherapy, delays the return of ovarian cancer compared with placebo (a look-alike tablet with no medicine). Zelparib is experimental and has not been approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

## What will happen and how long?

If you join, you will be **randomly assigned** — a 2 in 3 chance of Zelparib, 1 in 3 chance of placebo — to take one tablet by mouth twice daily. You will continue until your cancer progresses or side effects become unacceptable. You will attend clinic visits every 4 weeks and have a CT scan every 12 weeks. At each visit you will have a physical exam and a **blood draw of about 20 mL** (an invasive procedure).

## What are my responsibilities?

You should take the study drug twice daily, complete your symptom diary, attend all visits and scans, and tell the study team promptly about any new symptoms.

## What are the risks and discomforts?

Zelparib is **generally well tolerated, and most participants continue treatment without serious problems.** Some participants report mild nausea, tiredness, or reduced appetite that usually improve over time. As with any blood draw, you may have brief discomfort or a small bruise.

## Are there benefits?

Your cancer may stay in remission longer during the study, but this cannot be guaranteed, and you may receive placebo. The information learned may help future patients with ovarian cancer.

## What are my alternatives?

You do not have to join this study. Alternatives include routine monitoring (observation) after your chemotherapy, or other approved maintenance treatments if you are eligible. Your oncologist can discuss these options and their benefits and risks with you.

## What if I am injured?

If you are injured as a result of taking part in this study, medical care is available to you at the study site. Please tell the study team promptly so they can direct you to appropriate care.

## How will my information be kept confidential?

Records identifying you will be kept confidential and will not be made public; if results are published, your identity will remain confidential. Authorized monitors, auditors, the Institutional Review Board, and the FDA may review your original medical records to verify study procedures.

## Voluntary participation

Taking part is entirely voluntary. You may refuse to participate or withdraw at any time, without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled, and your regular cancer care will not be affected.
