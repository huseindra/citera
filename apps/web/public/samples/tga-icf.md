# Participant Information Sheet and Consent Form — Study AUV-330

**Study Title:** A Phase 2, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Corvalex in Adults with Uncontrolled Hypertension

**Sponsor:** Southern Cross Therapeutics Pty Ltd | **Consent Version:** 2.4 (Australian sites)

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## 1. Invitation and Purpose

You are invited to take part in a **research study**. This study involves research. Its **purpose** is to find out whether an investigational tablet called Corvalex lowers blood pressure in adults whose hypertension is not controlled by their current medicines, compared with placebo.

## 2. Study Treatments and Random Assignment

If you join, you will be **randomly assigned** — a **1 in 2 chance** — to receive Corvalex 25 mg or placebo, taken once daily alongside your usual blood pressure medicines. Corvalex is **experimental** and is not approved by the Therapeutic Goods Administration; taking it in this study is an experimental procedure.

## 3. Procedures and Duration

Your participation lasts about **12 weeks of treatment plus a follow-up call at Week 14**, with **six (6) clinic visits**. Visits include blood pressure checks, a 24-hour blood pressure monitor, an ECG, and a **blood draw of about 12 mL** (an invasive procedure).

## 4. Your Responsibilities

You are expected to take the study tablet daily, wear the blood pressure monitor as instructed, complete the medication diary, and attend all scheduled visits.

## 5. Risks and Discomforts

Corvalex has been **well tolerated** in previous studies. **Most participants experienced no side effects at all**, and **no serious or long-term side effects of Corvalex have been observed.** Some participants reported mild headache or tiredness that settled without treatment. Blood draws may cause brief discomfort or a small bruise.

## 6. Benefits

Your blood pressure control may improve during the study, though this cannot be guaranteed, and you may receive placebo. The results are expected to **benefit the wider community** of people living with hypertension.

## 7. Alternatives to Taking Part

You do not have to join this study to receive care. Alternatives include adjusting your current medicines, adding a different approved blood pressure medicine, or a structured lifestyle program. Your doctor can discuss the benefits and risks of each option.

## 8. What Happens if I Am Injured?

If you are injured as a result of taking part, **medical treatment will be provided and the sponsor will pay the reasonable costs** of treating research-related injuries, in line with the Medicines Australia compensation guidelines. **Services are available to participants adversely affected** by the research.

## 9. Payments and Expenses

You will receive **AU$50 per completed visit** for travel and inconvenience, pro-rated if you withdraw early. There is **no cost to you** for the study drug or study procedures. Payments are not intended to encourage you to take risks.

## 10. Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal

Taking part is entirely **voluntary**. You may **refuse to join or withdraw at any time without penalty** or loss of benefits, and your routine care will not be affected. If you stop the study drug early, the team will invite you to a **safety follow-up visit** so your health can be checked.

## 11. Privacy, Confidentiality, and Record Access

Your study records are **kept confidential**; if results are published, your identity will remain confidential. Authorized **monitors, auditors, the reviewing Human Research Ethics Committee (HREC), and the Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) will have direct access to your original medical records** to verify study conduct. By signing, you authorize this access.

## 12. New Information

You will be told **promptly about any new information** that might affect your willingness to continue in the study, and your consent may be sought again if the study changes.

## 13. Funding, Monitoring, and Interests

This study is **funded by Southern Cross Therapeutics Pty Ltd**. An independent **Data Safety Monitoring Board reviews safety data** during the study. The researchers **declare no personal financial interest** in the sponsor; their institutions are paid a set amount for each participant.

## 14. Results and Registration

The trial is **registered on the Australian New Zealand Clinical Trials Registry (ANZCTR)**. Results will be **published** in medical journals, and you may request a lay summary of the results and, after the study ends, be told which treatment you received.

## 15. Ending Participation, Duration, and Numbers

The study doctor may end your participation early for safety reasons, non-compliance, pregnancy, or if the study is stopped. About **220 people** will take part across eight Australian sites.
