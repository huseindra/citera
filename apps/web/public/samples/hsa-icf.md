# Participant Information Sheet and Consent Form — Study SGR-204

**Study Title:** A Phase 2, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Relvantra in Adults with Moderate COPD

**Sponsor:** Meridian Biotech Pte Ltd | **Consent Version:** 1.2 (Singapore sites)

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## 1. Invitation and Purpose

You are being invited to take part in a **research study**. This study involves research. Its **purpose** is to find out whether an investigational medicine called Relvantra improves lung function in adults with moderate COPD compared with placebo (a look-alike tablet with no medicine).

## 2. Study Treatments and Random Assignment

If you join, you will be **randomly assigned** — like tossing a coin, a **1 in 2 chance** — to receive either Relvantra 10 mg or placebo, taken as one tablet daily. Neither you nor your study doctor will know which one you receive. Relvantra is **experimental**: it has not been approved by the Health Sciences Authority or any other regulator, and taking it in this study is an experimental procedure.

## 3. Procedures and Duration

Your participation will last about **16 weeks of treatment plus a follow-up call**, with **five (5) clinic visits** (screening, baseline, Weeks 4, 10, and 16). At each visit you will have breathing tests (spirometry), vital signs, a physical examination, a symptom questionnaire, and a **blood draw of about 15 mL** (an invasive procedure) for safety and research tests.

## 4. Your Responsibilities

You should take the study tablet once daily, complete the daily electronic diary, attend all scheduled visits, and tell the study team before starting any new medicines.

## 5. Risks and Discomforts

Relvantra has been **well tolerated** in earlier studies. **Most participants experienced no side effects at all**, and **no serious or long-term side effects of Relvantra have been observed.** A few participants reported mild headache or dry mouth that resolved on their own. Blood draws may cause brief discomfort or a small bruise.

## 6. Benefits

Your lung function may improve during the study, but this cannot be guaranteed, and you may receive placebo. The information learned may help future patients with COPD.

## 7. Alternatives to Taking Part

You do not need to join this study to receive treatment. Alternatives include approved bronchodilator inhalers (LAMA/LABA), inhaled corticosteroid combinations, and pulmonary rehabilitation. Your doctor can discuss these options and their benefits and risks with you.

## 8. What Happens if I Am Injured?

If you are injured as a result of taking part in this study, **medical care is available** to you at the study site. Please inform the study team promptly so they can direct you to appropriate care.

## 9. Payment and Expenses

You will receive a transport and inconvenience allowance of **S$60 per completed visit**. If you withdraw early, the allowance is **pro-rated by the number of visits you completed**. There is **no cost to you** for the study drug or study procedures.

## 10. Voluntary Participation

Taking part is entirely **voluntary**. You may **refuse to participate or withdraw at any time, without penalty or loss of benefits** to which you are otherwise entitled, and your future medical care will not be affected.

## 11. Confidentiality and Access to Your Records

Records identifying you will be **kept confidential** and will not be made publicly available; if results are published, your identity will remain confidential. Authorized **monitors, auditors, the institutional review board, and the Health Sciences Authority (HSA) will be granted direct access to your original medical records** to verify study procedures, without violating your confidentiality. By signing, you authorize this access.

## 12. New Information and Ending Participation

You will be told **in a timely manner about any new information** that may be relevant to your willingness to continue. The study doctor may end your participation early for safety reasons, significant non-compliance, pregnancy, or if the study is stopped by the sponsor.

## 13. Number of Participants

About **180 people** will take part in this study across four sites in Singapore.
