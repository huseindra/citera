# Clinical Study Protocol — AUV-330

**Study Title:** A Phase 2, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Corvalex in Adults with Uncontrolled Hypertension

**Sponsor:** Southern Cross Therapeutics Pty Ltd | **Protocol Version:** 3.0 (CTN scheme)

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## 1. Objectives

To evaluate the efficacy of Corvalex 25 mg once daily versus placebo on 24-hour ambulatory systolic blood pressure over 12 weeks in adults with uncontrolled hypertension. Corvalex is an investigational product supplied under the Clinical Trial Notification (CTN) scheme; it is not approved by the TGA.

## 2. Study Design

Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial at eight Australian sites. Approximately **220 participants** will be randomized **1:1** to Corvalex or placebo, added to their existing antihypertensive regimen. Treatment period **12 weeks**; safety follow-up at Week 14.

## 3. Study Procedures

Participants attend **six (6) clinic visits**: screening, baseline/randomization, and Weeks 2, 6, 10, and 12, plus a Week 14 phone follow-up. Visits include blood pressure monitoring, ECG, vital signs, and a venous blood draw of approximately **12 mL** for safety laboratories.

## 4. Participant Responsibilities

Participants must take the study tablet daily, wear the ambulatory blood pressure monitor as instructed, complete the medication diary, and attend all visits.

## 5. Risks and Safety Considerations

In earlier studies, **elevated liver enzymes (ALT greater than 3× the upper limit of normal) occurred in approximately 3% of participants** on Corvalex, resolving after discontinuation; **liver function is monitored by blood test at every visit**. Symptomatic hypotension (dizziness, fainting) occurred in 6% of participants. Common adverse events include headache (9%) and fatigue (5%).

## 6. Benefits

Participants' blood pressure control may improve; benefit is not guaranteed, and half of participants receive placebo. Results are expected to benefit the wider community of patients with hypertension.

## 7. Alternative Treatments

Approved alternatives include optimizing current antihypertensives, adding an approved agent of a different class, and structured lifestyle programs.

## 8. Compensation for Injury

The sponsor subscribes to the Medicines Australia Guidelines for Compensation for Injury Resulting from Participation in a Company-Sponsored Clinical Trial. **The sponsor will pay the reasonable costs of treatment for trial-related injury**, and services are available to participants adversely affected by the research.

## 9. Funding, Monitoring, and Interests

The study is **funded by Southern Cross Therapeutics Pty Ltd**. An independent **Data Safety Monitoring Board reviews unblinded safety data** quarterly. Investigators receive per-participant payments to their institutions and declare no personal financial interests in the sponsor.

## 10. Payment and Expenses

Participants receive AU$50 per completed visit for travel and inconvenience, pro-rated on early withdrawal. Study drug and study procedures are provided at no cost.

## 11. Confidentiality and Record Access

Records identifying participants remain confidential. Authorized monitors, auditors, the reviewing HREC, and the **Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) may access original medical records** to verify study conduct.

## 12. Data Handling and Withdrawal

Data are identified by code number. **Data collected up to the point of withdrawal are retained** in the analysis; participants may request that no further data be collected. Blood samples of withdrawn participants are destroyed on request.

## 13. Dissemination

The trial is **registered on the Australian New Zealand Clinical Trials Registry (ANZCTR)**. Results will be published in peer-reviewed journals and a lay summary will be made available to participants on request.
