# Clinical Study Protocol — SGR-204

**Study Title:** A Phase 2, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Relvantra in Adults with Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

**Sponsor:** Meridian Biotech Pte Ltd, Singapore | **Protocol Version:** 2.1

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## 1. Objectives

To evaluate the efficacy of Relvantra 10 mg once daily versus placebo on lung function (trough FEV1) over 16 weeks in adults with moderate COPD, and to assess safety and tolerability. Relvantra is an investigational product; it has not been approved by any regulatory authority.

## 2. Study Design

Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group trial at four sites in Singapore. Approximately **180 participants** will be randomized **1:1** to Relvantra or matching placebo. The treatment period is **16 weeks** with a safety follow-up call two weeks after the last dose.

## 3. Study Procedures

Participants attend **five (5) clinic visits**: screening, baseline/randomization, and Weeks 4, 10, and 16. Each visit includes spirometry, vital signs, physical examination, a symptom questionnaire, and a venous blood draw of approximately **15 mL** for safety laboratories and pharmacokinetic (PK) analysis.

## 4. Biological Samples

PK and biomarker plasma samples are collected at baseline and Weeks 4, 10, and 16. **All PK and biomarker samples are shipped to the sponsor's central laboratory in San Diego, United States**, for analysis, and remaining aliquots are **stored for up to 5 years for future research** on respiratory disease. Sample analysis may generate incidental findings; site procedures for re-identification apply.

## 5. Participant Responsibilities

Participants must take the study tablet once daily, complete a daily e-diary, refrain from starting new respiratory medicines without consulting the investigator, and attend all scheduled visits.

## 6. Risks and Safety Considerations

In the Phase 1 program, **elevated liver enzymes (ALT/AST greater than 3× the upper limit of normal) occurred in approximately 4% of participants** receiving Relvantra and resolved on discontinuation. **Liver function is monitored by blood test at every visit.** One participant experienced a moderate hypersensitivity reaction (generalized urticaria). Common adverse events include headache (8%) and dry mouth (5%). Blood draws may cause bruising or discomfort.

## 7. Benefits

Participants may experience improved lung function; benefit is not guaranteed, and half of participants receive placebo. The study is expected to advance treatment options for COPD.

## 8. Alternative Treatments

Approved alternatives include long-acting bronchodilator inhalers (LAMA/LABA), inhaled corticosteroid combinations, and pulmonary rehabilitation. Participants do not need to join the study to receive standard care.

## 9. Compensation for Injury

The sponsor maintains clinical trial insurance. For a trial-related injury, **the sponsor will pay the reasonable costs of medical treatment**, in accordance with the site's compensation guidelines.

## 10. Payment and Expenses

Participants receive a transport and inconvenience allowance of S$60 per completed visit, **pro-rated by the number of visits completed** if they withdraw early. There is no charge to participants for the study drug or study procedures.

## 11. Confidentiality and Record Access

Study records identifying participants are kept confidential. Authorized monitors, auditors, the institutional review board, and the **Health Sciences Authority (HSA) may inspect original medical records** to verify study conduct.

## 12. Withdrawal and Termination

Participation is voluntary. The investigator may terminate a participant's involvement for safety reasons, significant non-compliance, pregnancy, or if the sponsor stops the study. Participants are informed promptly of new information relevant to their willingness to continue.
