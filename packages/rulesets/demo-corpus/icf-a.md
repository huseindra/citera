# Informed Consent Form — Study CTX-101 (Version A)

**Study Title:** A Phase 2, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Centraxol in Adults with Moderate Persistent Asthma

**Sponsor:** Aruna Therapeutics, Inc. | **Consent Version:** A-1.0

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## Introduction

You are being asked to take part in a research study. This form explains the study so you can decide whether to participate. Please read it carefully and ask the study team any questions before you sign.

## Why is this study being done?

This study involves research. Its purpose is to find out whether an investigational medicine called Centraxol improves lung function and asthma control in adults with moderate persistent asthma when added to their usual inhaled corticosteroid, compared with placebo (a look-alike tablet with no medicine). Centraxol is experimental: it has not been approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), and taking it in this study is an experimental procedure.

## How long will I take part, and what will happen?

Your participation will last about 24 weeks of treatment. You will attend **six (6) scheduled clinic visits**: a screening visit, a baseline visit where you are randomly assigned (2 in 3 chance of Centraxol, 1 in 3 chance of placebo), and visits at Weeks 4, 12, 18, and 24. About two weeks after your last dose, the study team will call you for a safety follow-up.

At each clinic visit you will have:

- Breathing tests (spirometry), repeated three times
- Vital signs and a physical examination
- A blood draw of about 20 mL (about 4 teaspoons) for safety tests, including liver function tests, and research biomarkers
- An asthma control questionnaire
- A review of your daily electronic symptom diary

You will take one tablet of study drug by mouth once daily and continue your prescribed inhaler. Neither you nor the study doctor will know whether you receive Centraxol or placebo.

## What are the risks and discomforts?

Centraxol may cause side effects. In earlier studies, the following were seen:

- **Headache** — about 12 out of 100 participants; usually mild and short-lasting.
- **Nausea** — about 8 out of 100 participants, most often in the first two weeks.
- **Elevated liver enzymes** — about 3 out of 100 participants had blood-test abnormalities suggesting liver irritation, without symptoms, which resolved after stopping the drug. Because of this risk, **your liver function will be checked by a blood test at every visit**, and you must stop the study drug if the elevations are confirmed.
- **Severe allergic reaction** — a serious allergic reaction (swelling of the face and airway) occurred in one participant in an earlier study. Although rare, a severe allergic reaction can be life-threatening. Tell the study team immediately about any rash, swelling, or trouble breathing.

Study procedures also carry risks: blood draws may cause discomfort, bruising, or rarely infection; spirometry may cause temporary lightheadedness or shortness of breath.

There may be side effects that are not yet known, including unknown risks to an unborn child. If you can become pregnant, you must use an acceptable method of contraception during the study.

## Are there benefits?

Your asthma control may improve during the study, but this cannot be guaranteed, and you may receive placebo. The information learned may help future patients with asthma.

## What are my alternatives?

You do not have to join this study to receive treatment for your asthma. Alternatives include continuing or adjusting your current inhaled corticosteroid, adding a long-acting bronchodilator (a combination inhaler), leukotriene tablets, or, if appropriate for you, approved biologic injections. Your doctor can discuss these options with you.

## How will my information be kept confidential?

Your study records will be labeled with a code number rather than your name. Records that identify you will be seen only by the study team, the sponsor's authorized monitors, the Institutional Review Board (IRB), and regulatory authorities, **including the FDA, which may inspect study records**. Study data are kept on secure, access-controlled systems. Total confidentiality cannot be absolutely guaranteed, but every reasonable safeguard is in place.

## What happens if I am injured?

If you are injured as a direct result of taking part in this study, **medical treatment is available, and the sponsor, Aruna Therapeutics, will pay the reasonable costs of treating the injury** to the extent they are not covered by your insurance. **No other compensation — for example for lost wages or discomfort — is offered.** For more information about care and compensation for research-related injury, contact the study doctor at the number below.

## Is participation voluntary?

**Yes. Taking part in this study is entirely voluntary.** You may decline to participate, and you may stop participating at any time, for any reason, **without penalty and without losing any benefits or medical care you are otherwise entitled to**. If you decide to withdraw, tell the study team so they can advise a safe stopping plan. The study doctor may also withdraw you for safety reasons, such as confirmed liver-test elevations.
