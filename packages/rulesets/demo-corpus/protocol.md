# Clinical Study Protocol CTX-101

**Protocol Title:** A Phase 2, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Centraxol in Adults with Moderate Persistent Asthma

**Protocol Number:** CTX-101 | **Version:** 2.0 | **Sponsor:** Aruna Therapeutics, Inc.

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes. Centraxol, Aruna Therapeutics, and all study details are fictional.*

## 1. Background and Rationale

Moderate persistent asthma affects a substantial adult population whose symptoms remain inadequately controlled on inhaled corticosteroids alone. Centraxol is an investigational oral selective inflammation-pathway modulator. In the completed Phase 1 program (64 healthy volunteers) and a Phase 1b study (28 patients), Centraxol showed dose-dependent reduction of airway inflammation markers. This study evaluates the efficacy and safety of Centraxol as add-on therapy in adults with moderate persistent asthma.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary objective:** To evaluate the effect of Centraxol 40 mg once daily versus placebo on change in pre-bronchodilator FEV1 from baseline to Week 24.

**Secondary objectives:** Asthma Control Questionnaire (ACQ-6) score change; rescue inhaler use; safety and tolerability.

## 3. Study Design

This is a randomized (2:1 Centraxol:placebo), double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group study. The treatment period is 24 weeks.

Participation involves **six (6) scheduled clinic visits** — Screening (Visit 1), Baseline/Randomization (Visit 2), and follow-up visits at Weeks 4, 12, 18, and 24 (Visits 3–6) — plus one safety telephone follow-up approximately 2 weeks after the last dose.

## 4. Study Population

Approximately 180 adults aged 18–65 with physician-diagnosed moderate persistent asthma for at least 12 months, on a stable medium-dose inhaled corticosteroid regimen.

Key exclusion criteria include: current smoking, hepatic impairment (ALT or AST greater than 1.5× the upper limit of normal at screening), pregnancy or breastfeeding, and hospitalization for asthma within 3 months of screening.

## 5. Study Procedures

At each clinic visit, participants undergo:

- Spirometry (lung function testing), performed in triplicate
- Vital signs and physical examination
- **Blood sampling of approximately 20 mL per visit** for safety laboratory tests (including liver function tests) and exploratory biomarkers
- Asthma Control Questionnaire (ACQ-6)
- Review of the participant's electronic daily symptom diary and rescue inhaler use

Study drug (Centraxol 40 mg or matching placebo) is taken orally once daily. Participants continue their prescribed inhaled corticosteroid throughout the study.

## 6. Risks and Safety Considerations

The following risks of Centraxol have been identified in the completed Phase 1 and Phase 1b studies:

- **Headache** — reported by approximately **12%** of participants; generally mild and transient.
- **Nausea** — reported by approximately **8%** of participants, most commonly during the first two weeks of dosing.
- **Elevated liver enzymes (ALT/AST)** — asymptomatic elevations greater than 3× the upper limit of normal observed in approximately **3%** of participants, reversible upon discontinuation. **Because of this identified hepatic risk, liver function is monitored by blood testing at every study visit**, and the protocol mandates discontinuation rules for confirmed elevations.
- **Hypersensitivity reactions** — one case of a **serious hypersensitivity reaction (angioedema)** was reported in the Phase 1b study. Although rare, severe allergic reaction is a recognized potential risk and is described in the Investigator's Brochure.

Risks of study procedures include discomfort, bruising, or rarely infection at the blood-draw site, and temporary lightheadedness or shortness of breath during spirometry.

There may be additional risks of Centraxol that are currently unknown, including unknown risks to an unborn child; contraception requirements apply.

## 7. Potential Benefits

Participants may experience improved asthma control, although no benefit is guaranteed; participants randomized to placebo receive no active investigational treatment. Information from this study may benefit future patients with asthma.

## 8. Alternatives to Participation

Eligible patients do not need to join this study to receive treatment. Appropriate alternatives available through routine care include continuing or adjusting inhaled corticosteroid therapy, adding a long-acting bronchodilator (ICS/LABA combination), leukotriene receptor antagonists, and, for eligible patients, approved biologic therapies. These options should be discussed with the treating physician.

## 9. Confidentiality and Data Handling

Participant records are identified by a coded study number. Access to identifiable records is limited to the study team, the sponsor's authorized monitors, the reviewing Institutional Review Board (IRB), and regulatory authorities **including the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), which may inspect study records**. Study data are stored on access-controlled systems.

## 10. Care and Compensation for Research-Related Injury

If a participant is injured as a direct result of study participation, **the sponsor (Aruna Therapeutics) will pay the reasonable costs of medical treatment for the injury**, to the extent the costs are not covered by the participant's insurance. **No additional financial compensation (for example, for lost wages or discomfort) is provided.** The informed consent form must explain both the availability of medical treatment and this compensation position, and identify where participants can obtain further information.

## 11. Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal

Participation is voluntary. Participants may decline to participate or withdraw at any time without penalty and without affecting their regular medical care. The investigator may withdraw a participant for safety reasons, including confirmed liver enzyme elevations meeting the protocol's discontinuation criteria.

## 12. Contacts

Questions regarding the study are directed to the site Principal Investigator. Questions regarding participant rights are directed to the reviewing IRB. The 24-hour medical contact for suspected study-related injury is the site's on-call study physician.
