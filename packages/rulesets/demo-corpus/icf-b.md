# Informed Consent Form — Study CTX-101 (Version B)

**Study Title:** A Phase 2, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study of Centraxol in Adults with Moderate Persistent Asthma

**Sponsor:** Aruna Therapeutics, Inc. | **Consent Version:** B-0.9 (draft)

*This is a fully synthetic document created for demonstration purposes.*

## Introduction

You are being asked to take part in a research study. This form explains the study so you can decide whether to participate. Please read it carefully and ask the study team any questions before you sign.

## Why is this study being done?

This study involves research. Its purpose is to find out whether an investigational medicine called Centraxol improves lung function and asthma control in adults with moderate persistent asthma when added to their usual inhaled corticosteroid, compared with placebo (a look-alike tablet with no medicine). Centraxol is experimental: it has not been approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), and taking it in this study is an experimental procedure.

## How long will I take part, and what will happen?

Your participation will last about 24 weeks of treatment. You will attend **six (6) scheduled clinic visits**: a screening visit, a baseline visit where you are randomly assigned (2 in 3 chance of Centraxol, 1 in 3 chance of placebo), and visits at Weeks 4, 12, 18, and 24. About two weeks after your last dose, the study team will call you for a safety follow-up.

At each clinic visit you will have breathing tests (spirometry), vital signs and a physical examination, a blood draw of about 20 mL for laboratory tests and research biomarkers, an asthma control questionnaire, and a review of your daily electronic symptom diary.

You will take one tablet of study drug by mouth once daily and continue your prescribed inhaler. Neither you nor the study doctor will know whether you receive Centraxol or placebo.

## What are the risks and discomforts?

Centraxol has been **well tolerated** in previous studies. **Most participants experienced no side effects at all.** A small number of participants reported **mild headache or brief nausea, which went away on their own** within a few days. **No serious or long-term side effects of Centraxol have been observed.**

As with any blood draw, you may have brief discomfort or a small bruise.

## Are there benefits?

Your asthma control may improve during the study, but this cannot be guaranteed, and you may receive placebo. The information learned may help future patients with asthma.

## What are my alternatives?

You do not have to join this study to receive treatment for your asthma. Alternatives include continuing or adjusting your current inhaled corticosteroid, adding a long-acting bronchodilator (a combination inhaler), leukotriene tablets, or, if appropriate for you, approved biologic injections. Your doctor can discuss these options with you.

## How will my information be kept confidential?

Your study records will be labeled with a code number rather than your name. Records that identify you will be seen only by the study team, the sponsor's authorized monitors, the Institutional Review Board (IRB), and regulatory authorities, **including the FDA, which may inspect study records**. Study data are kept on secure, access-controlled systems. Total confidentiality cannot be absolutely guaranteed, but every reasonable safeguard is in place.

## What happens if I feel unwell during the study?

If you feel unwell at any point during the study, please tell the study team. **Medical care is available at the study clinic during your scheduled visits**, and the study staff can help direct you to appropriate care between visits.
