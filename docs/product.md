# Product

## Vision

Build the most trustworthy AI platform for reviewing clinical trial documents.

Unlike traditional AI assistants that generate answers with limited transparency, this platform enables reviewers to inspect the complete evidence behind every AI-generated finding.

The platform should help regulatory teams review documents faster while maintaining confidence, traceability, and compliance.

---

# Problem

Clinical trial document review is manual, repetitive, and difficult to verify.

Reviewers must compare multiple documents, including:

* Study Protocol
* Informed Consent Form (ICF)
* Regulatory Requirements
* Supporting Guidance

Finding inconsistencies requires constant cross-referencing, making the process slow and prone to human error.

Current AI tools summarize documents but rarely explain how conclusions are reached.

In regulated environments, unsupported conclusions are not enough.

Every recommendation must be backed by evidence.

---

# Mission

Build an AI-native Evidence Intelligence platform that makes every AI decision transparent, explainable, and auditable.

The platform should assist reviewers rather than replace them.

Every finding must be independently verifiable.

---

# Target Users

Primary Users

* Regulatory Affairs
* Clinical Research Associates
* Clinical Research Organizations (CRO)
* Institutional Review Boards (IRB)

Secondary Users

* Sponsors
* Quality Assurance
* Clinical Operations

---

# Core Value Proposition

The product should enable reviewers to answer these questions within seconds:

* What is wrong?
* Why is it wrong?
* Which regulation applies?
* Which protocol section supports this?
* Which evidence was retrieved?
* How confident is the AI?
* Can I verify this independently?

---

# Core Features

## Clinical Document Review

Automatically analyze:

* Study Protocols
* Informed Consent Forms
* Supporting Clinical Documents

---

## Regulation Validation

Validate document completeness against configurable regulatory frameworks.

Initial support:

* FDA 21 CFR Part 50
* Common Rule (45 CFR 46)

Future support:

* ICH-GCP
* EMA
* WHO
* BPOM

---

## Evidence Heatmap

Visualize semantic confidence throughout a document.

Highlight paragraphs based on evidence strength.

Users should instantly identify sections requiring attention.

---

## Semantic Evidence Map

Represent relationships between:

* Regulations
* Protocol Sections
* ICF Sections
* Retrieved Evidence
* AI Findings

The interface should reveal how evidence flows through the reasoning process.

---

## Explainable Retrieval

Never hide retrieval.

Every finding should expose:

* Retrieved chunks
* Similarity score
* Supporting regulation
* Protocol references
* Confidence score

---

## Citation Graph

Display relationships between:

Regulation

↓

Protocol

↓

Evidence

↓

Finding

↓

Recommendation

Users should navigate evidence instead of reading flat citation lists.

---

## Audit Replay

Every AI decision should be reproducible.

Allow reviewers to inspect:

* Retrieved context
* Prompt context
* Evidence chain
* Generated finding

---

# Product Principles

The platform should always prioritize:

* Trust over automation
* Evidence over assumptions
* Transparency over convenience
* Explainability over complexity
* Human oversight over autonomous decisions

---

# Non Goals

This product is NOT:

* A chatbot
* A medical diagnosis system
* An autonomous clinical reviewer
* An electronic trial master file (eTMF)
* A document management system

The focus is evidence-driven AI review.

---

# Success Metrics

The product succeeds when reviewers can:

* Review documents significantly faster.
* Understand every AI recommendation.
* Verify every finding independently.
* Reduce manual cross-referencing.
* Trust AI-generated recommendations through observable evidence.

---

# Guiding Principle

The platform should never ask users to trust the model.

The platform should help users trust the evidence.
